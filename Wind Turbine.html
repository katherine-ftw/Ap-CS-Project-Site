Kidwind Challenge

The KidWind Challenge is a hands-on design competition where student teams showcase their small-scale wind turbines and solar home designs with the primary goal of providing a space for students to demonstrate their knowledge, skill, creativity, and passion for clean energy to their communities, industry, and the wider public.

Our Wind Turbine

For our wind turbine, we designed one with 3d printed blades and the original gear ratio. I personally worked on testing the different gear ratios and stuck to the 64:8 ratio. We learned (at least I did) how to measure amps and voltage for these turbines using a multimeter and how to use this information to calculate power. Some challenges that I encountered was the multimeter that wasn't working properly, causing some confusion. Another challenge that we faced was that the blades kept breaking. So, to combat this issue, we ended up soldering the different parts of the blade together. The original wind turbine produced 1.9V but our improved wind turbine produced 2.78V.
